Troubleshooting Guide

The most common problems beginners hit ‚Äî and exactly how to fix them. Fast.

7 Common Issues
üìû

AI Voice Calls Are Dropping

Calls
‚ñº
‚ö° Why It Happens

Usually a connection timeout between GHL and your AI voice platform. The API call goes out but the voice tool doesn't respond in time.

‚úÖ FIX

Go to Settings ‚Üí AI Voice ‚Üí Connection and increase the timeout to 10 seconds. Also check that your API key hasn't expired ‚Äî regenerate it if it's older than 30 days. If calls still drop, switch to VAPI which has the most stable GHL connection.

üì±

SMS Messages Not Delivering

SMS
‚ñº
‚ö° Why It Happens

SMS delivery fails when: (1) the guest's number format is wrong (missing country code), (2) your GHL phone number isn't verified, or (3) you've hit your daily SMS limit.

‚úÖ FIX

Always store numbers in international format (+1 for US, +44 for UK, etc.). Go to Settings ‚Üí Phone Numbers and verify your sending number. Check Dashboard ‚Üí SMS Credits ‚Äî if you're at 0, add credits before the next send.

‚öôÔ∏è

Workflow Not Triggering

Workflow
‚ñº
‚ö° Why It Happens

The #1 cause: the workflow is saved as a Draft instead of being published. Second most common: the trigger condition doesn't match what's actually happening (e.g., trigger set to "Booking Confirmed" but bookings come in as "Lead Created").

‚úÖ FIX

Open your workflow ‚Üí check the status toggle in the top-right. It must say Published (green). Then go to the trigger step and verify the exact event name matches your incoming data. Use the Test Mode button to manually fire the trigger and watch it execute step by step.

üîó

AI Voice Integration Failing

Setup
‚ñº
‚ö° Why It Happens

API key is wrong, expired, or you're using a test key instead of a live key. Some voice platforms require an extra "activation" step before the API key works.

‚úÖ FIX

Log into your voice platform dashboard. Delete your current API key and generate a brand new one. Make sure you select "Live" (not Test) when creating it. Copy it fresh ‚Üí paste into GHL ‚Üí hit the green Test Connection button. If it still fails, check that your voice platform account is on a paid plan (free tiers sometimes block API access).

üë§

Guest Names Showing as [blank]

Workflow
‚ñº
‚ö° Why It Happens

The dynamic variable [Guest Name] pulls from the CRM contact record. If the contact was created without a first name (common with auto-imports), the field is empty and shows as blank in your voice script.

‚úÖ FIX

Add a step before your AI voice call that checks if "First Name" is empty. If it is, add a fallback ‚Äî either a default like "Valued Guest" or trigger an SMS asking for their name first. In GHL: Add Step ‚Üí Condition ‚Üí If First Name is Empty ‚Üí Set to "Guest".

üì¨

Emails Going to Spam

Email
‚ñº
‚ö° Why It Happens

GHL sends from a generic domain by default. Email providers flag this as spam because there's no SPF/DKIM authentication matching your property's brand.

‚úÖ FIX

Connect your own Google Workspace email to GHL under Settings ‚Üí Email ‚Üí Custom Domain. Then set up SPF and DKIM records (GHL gives you the exact records to copy). This tells email providers "yes, this email is really from us." Takes about 15 minutes and fixes 90% of spam issues.

üîÑ

Automation Running Multiple Times

Workflow
‚ñº
‚ö° Why It Happens

The workflow has no "run once" limit. Every time the trigger fires (even if it's the same contact), the whole flow repeats ‚Äî sending duplicate messages to the guest.

‚úÖ FIX

Open the workflow ‚Üí click Settings (gear icon) ‚Üí enable "Run Once Per Contact". This ensures each guest only goes through the flow once. If you need it to run again (e.g., for a return guest), you can manually re-enroll them or set a time-based reset (e.g., "allow again after 90 days").

New ¬∑ FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

The real questions beginners ask after reading the guides. Honest, straight answers ‚Äî no runaround.

12 Questions Answered
‚ùì
Can I use GHL without a website?
‚ñº

Yes ‚Äî 100%. GHL has a built-in website builder, but you don't even need to use it. Most hospitality automations run purely through SMS, email, and AI voice calls. No website required to get started.

If you already have a property listing on Booking.com or Airbnb, that's all you need as your "front door." GHL works behind the scenes.

‚ùì
Does AI voice work internationally?
‚ñº

Yes, but it depends on the platform. VAPI and Retell AI both support international calls and multilingual voices. Bland AI is more limited to English-dominant calls.

For properties that receive guests from many countries, Retell AI is the best pick ‚Äî it has the strongest multilingual support (Spanish, French, German, Mandarin, and more). Just make sure you store guest phone numbers with the correct country code.

‚ùì
How many automations can I run at once?
‚ñº

On the Unlimited and Agency plans ‚Äî unlimited. There's no cap on how many workflows you can create or run simultaneously. You could have 50 active automations running across different guest segments at the same time.

The Starter plan has some limits on advanced workflow features, but even it can handle multiple basic automations running in parallel.

‚ùì
What if a guest wants to talk to a real person?
‚ñº

Build an "escape hatch" into your AI voice script. End every call with: "Press 0 to speak with a team member." When they press 0, the AI voice call ends and GHL can auto-dial your front desk or send you an alert SMS.

This is standard practice. Guests feel safe knowing the option exists, even if 80% never use it.

‚ùì
Is GHL worth it for just one property?
‚ñº

It depends on your revenue. If your property earns $5,000+/mo, GHL pays for itself easily ‚Äî even one saved staff hour per day adds up fast. The automation alone can replace a part-time front desk assistant.

If you're smaller, start on Starter ($97/mo) and upgrade as you grow. The ROI comes from fewer missed bookings, faster guest responses, and better reviews (which drive more bookings).

‚ùì
Can I cancel GHL anytime?
‚ñº

Yes. GHL is month-to-month ‚Äî no annual contracts required. You can cancel anytime from your account settings. Your data and automations stay in the system until the end of your billing period.

Before canceling, export your contact list (GHL allows CSV export) so you don't lose your guest database.

‚ùì
Do I need technical skills to set this up?
‚ñº

No coding required. GHL is built for non-technical users ‚Äî it's drag-and-drop for workflows, and the AI voice connection is just copying and pasting an API key. If you can use a smartphone and follow step-by-step instructions, you can set this up.

The hardest part most beginners face is writing good AI voice scripts. But even that is just writing a few sentences ‚Äî no tech knowledge needed.

‚ùì
What happens if the AI voice makes a mistake?
‚ñº

AI voice tools occasionally stumble on unusual names or mispronounce words. To minimize this: (1) Keep scripts short and simple. (2) Preview every script before going live. (3) Use phonetic spelling for tricky property names (e.g., "The Mah-JEE-kah Resort").

If a guest reports an issue, you'll get flagged in GHL. You can manually follow up with a personal message to smooth it over. It's rare, and guests are generally understanding.

‚ùì
Can guests reply to AI voice calls?
‚ñº

Yes ‚Äî using keypad input. You can set up "Press 1 for X, Press 2 for Y" menus. The AI captures their keypress and routes them accordingly inside GHL.

Full conversational replies (guest speaking freely) are possible with Retell AI, which has the best natural language understanding. But for most hospitality uses, keypad menus are sufficient and more reliable.

‚ùì
How do I handle different languages?
‚ñº

GHL itself supports multiple languages for SMS and email. For AI voice, Retell AI is the strongest multilingual option. You can create separate voice scripts in different languages and set up logic: if the guest's location is France ‚Üí send the French script.

Start with English only. Add languages based on where your actual guests come from ‚Äî don't over-engineer it from day one.

‚ùì
What's the difference between GHL and a PMS?
‚ñº

A PMS (Property Management System) like Cloudbeds or Mews handles the actual hotel operations ‚Äî room inventory, check-in/out, housekeeping. GHL handles guest communication and marketing automations.

They work best together. Your PMS manages the property; GHL manages the guest relationship. Many hosts connect their PMS to GHL so booking data flows automatically between both systems.

‚ùì
Is there a mobile app for GHL?
‚ñº

Yes ‚Äî GHL has iOS and Android apps. You can monitor your automations, see guest activity, and respond to messages on the go. However, building and editing workflows is still easier on desktop.

Recommended workflow: Set everything up on desktop first. Then use the mobile app for daily monitoring and quick guest responses while you're on the move.

ExampleWhen a guest books a room, a new CRM contact is automatically created with their name, email, phone number, and booking date.

‚ñº

A visual board (like Kanban) that shows where each guest is in their journey ‚Äî from "Inquiry" to "Booked" to "Checked In." Think of it as columns that guests move through automatically.

ExampleA guest submits a booking ‚Üí moves to "Confirmed" column ‚Üí checks in ‚Üí moves to "Active Stay" column. Each move can trigger a different automation.
Contact / Lead
‚ñº

A person in your CRM. "Lead" usually means someone who hasn't booked yet (just showed interest). "Contact" is the broader term for anyone in the system ‚Äî leads, guests, past visitors.

ExampleSomeone fills out your website inquiry form ‚Üí becomes a "Lead." Once they book a stay, they become a "Contact" with a full guest profile.
Tag
‚ñº

Labels you add to a contact to organize and filter them. Tags are how GHL knows who to send what message to. You can create any tags you want.

ExampleTags like "VIP Guest," "Checked In," "Needs Follow-Up," or "Spa Interest" help you segment guests and trigger the right automations.
Workflow
‚ñº

A set of automatic steps that run when something happens. A workflow has a Trigger (what starts it) and Actions (what it does). Think of it as a "if this happens, then do that" chain.

ExampleTrigger: "Guest books a room" ‚Üí Action 1: Send welcome SMS ‚Üí Action 2: Wait 1 hour ‚Üí Action 3: Send AI voice call.
Trigger
‚ñº

The event that starts a workflow. Nothing happens until the trigger fires. Triggers can be time-based ("2 hours before check-in") or action-based ("new booking confirmed").

ExampleCommon hospitality triggers: New booking, check-in date approaching, guest checks out, guest replies to SMS.
Drip Campaign
‚ñº

A series of messages sent automatically over time ‚Äî one after another at set intervals. "Drip" because the messages "drip" out slowly, not all at once.

ExampleDay 1: Welcome email. Day 3: "Here's what to do at our property" SMS. Day 5: Spa offer. Day 7: Review request after checkout.
